## Terms and Definitions

### A

#### **Activator**
A transcription factor that binds to a regulatory element and increases the probability of transcription.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Regulation
- **Related Terms**: transcription factor, regulatory element, promoter, repressor

#### **Active Site**
A region of an enzyme that binds a substrate during a reaction.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Enzymes
- **Related Terms**: enzyme, substrate, catalyst, chemical reaction

#### **Active Transport (Primary and Secondary)**
When a protein uses energy to move a specific type of solute from a region of low concentration to a region of high concentration.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: primary active transport, secondary active transport, concentration gradient, carrier protein

#### **Amphiphilic**
A molecule having a nonpolar, hydrophobic region and a polar, hydrophilic region.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Molecular Properties
- **Related Terms**: hydrophobic, hydrophilic, cell membrane

#### **Amplification**
An increase in the intensity of a signal caused by the activation of multiple enzymes at each step of a signaling pathway.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: signaling pathway, signal transduction, cellular response

#### **Antiporter**
A carrier protein that binds two types of solutes and changes conformation to move one solute into the cell and the other solute out of the cell.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: carrier protein, symporter, active transport

#### **Atom**
The smallest unit of matter that can participate in a chemical reaction, consisting of one or more protons and some number of electrons; atoms are categorized into types called elements.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Basic Concepts
- **Related Terms**: molecule, bond, chemical reaction

### B

#### **Bond**
An attraction that holds two atoms together.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Basic Concepts
- **Related Terms**: covalent bond, ionic bond, hydrogen bond, atom

### C

#### **Carrier Protein**
A protein that binds a specific type of solute and transports this solute through a cell membrane by changing conformation.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: antiporter, symporter, active transport, facilitated diffusion

#### **Catalyst**
A substance that increases the rate of a reaction without itself being consumed by that reaction. An enzyme catalyzes a reaction.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Enzymes
- **Related Terms**: enzyme, chemical reaction, active site

#### **Cell**
The smallest system of matter and energy that regulates its own environment to grow and reproduce.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Basic Concepts
- **Related Terms**: cell theory, cell membrane, nucleus, cytoplasm

#### **Cell Membrane**
A fluid barrier that prevents certain solutes from passing into or out of a cell.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Cell Structure
- **Related Terms**: amphiphilic, permeability, channel protein, carrier protein

#### **Cell Theory**
All living things consist of cells.
- **Category**: Biology, Fundamental Theories
- **Related Terms**: cell, evolutionary theory, germ theory

#### **Cellular Response**
A change in activities or concentrations of proteins caused by a signaling molecule.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: signaling pathway, signal transduction, receptor

#### **Channel Protein**
A protein that forms a pore in the cell membrane, through which a specific type of solute can diffuse.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: facilitated diffusion, cell membrane, permeability

#### **Chemical Affinity**
The likelihood that two molecules will interact, stemming from the electrical properties of their atoms. The greater the affinity between molecules, the greater the strength of an interaction.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Molecular Interactions
- **Related Terms**: chemical specificity, ligand, receptor

#### **Chemical Reaction**
A process in which molecules interact to form new molecules with different compositions.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Basic Concepts
- **Related Terms**: catalyst, enzyme, substrate, product

#### **Chemical Specificity**
The ability of a protein to bind a specific type of molecules; the fewer types of molecules a protein can bind, the greater its specificity.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Protein Function
- **Related Terms**: chemical affinity, ligand, receptor

#### **Claim**
An assertion about truth.
- **Category**: Scientific Method
- **Related Terms**: evidence, reasoning, scientific argument

#### **Codon**
A sequence of three nitrogenous bases that codes for a specific amino acid.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: genetic code, mRNA, translation, tRNA

#### **Concentration**
The number of particles that occur in a certain volume.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Basic Concepts
- **Related Terms**: concentration gradient, osmolarity, diffusion

#### **Concentration Gradient**
A difference in concentration between two regions of space.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Transport
- **Related Terms**: diffusion, net diffusion, active transport

#### **Covalent Bond**
A strong attraction that occurs when two atoms share electrons.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Chemical Bonds
- **Related Terms**: bond, molecule, peptide bond

#### **Cytoplasm**
The fluid between the nucleus and the cell membrane.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Cell Structure
- **Related Terms**: cell, nucleoplasm, cell membrane

### D

#### **Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA)**
A nucleic acid in which each nucleotide has deoxyribose as its carbohydrate.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: nucleic acid, RNA, nucleotide, gene

#### **Dependent Variable**
A variable whose expected value depends on the value of another variable.
- **Category**: Statistics, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: independent variable, relationship, linear relationship

#### **Dephosphorylation**
A reaction that removes a phosphate group from a molecule.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: phosphorylation, phosphatase, kinase

#### **Diffusion**
The random movement of a particle in space caused by collisions with other particles.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Transport
- **Related Terms**: net diffusion, facilitated diffusion, Fick's equation

#### **Direct Effect**
A causal relationship between two variables modeled without an intermediate variable.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: indirect effect, path model, variable

### E

#### **Effector**
A component that affects the value of the regulated variable.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Homeostasis
- **Related Terms**: homeostatic system, sensor, integrator

#### **Electrochemical Gradient**
A combination of a concentration gradient and an electrical gradient, caused by an imbalance of ions between two regions.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Transport
- **Related Terms**: concentration gradient, secondary active transport, ion transport

#### **Enzyme**
A protein that speeds a chemical reaction by reducing the energy needed for the reaction to occur.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Protein Function
- **Related Terms**: catalyst, active site, substrate, kinase, phosphatase

#### **Evidence**
Information related to the accuracy of a claim.
- **Category**: Scientific Method
- **Related Terms**: claim, reasoning, scientific argument

#### **Evolutionary Theory**
All organisms on Earth descended from a common ancestor.
- **Category**: Biology, Fundamental Theories
- **Related Terms**: cell theory, germ theory, mutation

### F

#### **Facilitated Diffusion**
The diffusion of a solute through a protein in the cell membrane.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: diffusion, channel protein, carrier protein

#### **Fick's Equation**
An equation relating the rate of net diffusion to the chemical properties of the solutes and the physical properties of a membrane.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Transport Physics
- **Related Terms**: diffusion, net diffusion, permeability

#### **Frequency**
The number of times that a value within a range has been observed.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: frequency distribution, relative frequency

#### **Frequency Distribution**
Enables one to predict the probability of observing specific values of a variable.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: frequency, mean, standard deviation

### G

#### **Gene**
A set of chemical instructions for producing a specific type of peptide.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: DNA, gene expression, transcription, translation

#### **Gene Expression**
The process by which a cell uses information encoded in a gene to produce proteins.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: gene, transcription, translation, protein

#### **Genetic Code**
The set of 64 codons that correspond to the 20 amino acids of proteins.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: codon, mRNA, translation, amino acid

#### **Germ Theory**
Microorganisms can cause disease.
- **Category**: Biology, Fundamental Theories
- **Related Terms**: cell theory, evolutionary theory

### H

#### **Homeostasis**
The process of regulating the value of an internal variable in response to changes caused by external factors.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Physiology
- **Related Terms**: homeostatic system, negative feedback, set point

#### **Homeostatic System**
A set of components that work together as a network to regulate the value of a variable.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Physiology
- **Related Terms**: homeostasis, sensor, integrator, effector

#### **Hormone**
A signaling molecule that circulates through the blood or a similar fluid.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling, Physiology
- **Related Terms**: signaling molecule, receptor, cellular response

#### **Hydrogen Bond**
A weak attraction between a hydrogen atom in one molecule and an atom with a partial negative charge in another molecule.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Chemical Bonds
- **Related Terms**: bond, water, hydrophilic

#### **Hydrophilic**
Having an attraction to molecules of water.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Molecular Properties
- **Related Terms**: hydrophobic, amphiphilic, hydrogen bond

#### **Hydrophobic**
Having no attraction to molecules of water.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Molecular Properties
- **Related Terms**: hydrophilic, amphiphilic, lipid

### I

#### **Independent Variable**
A variable whose expected value remains independent of the values of other variables.
- **Category**: Statistics, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: dependent variable, relationship, experiment

#### **Indirect Effect**
A causal relationship between two variables mediated by an intermediate variable.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: direct effect, path model, variable

#### **Integration**
A process in which two or more signaling pathways converge to influence a cell's response.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: signaling pathway, cellular response, signal transduction

#### **Integrator**
A component that combines information from sensors and activates a component that changes the value of the regulated variable.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Homeostasis
- **Related Terms**: homeostatic system, sensor, effector

#### **Intercept**
A parameter that defines the value of the dependent variable when the value of the independent variable equals zero.
- **Category**: Statistics, Linear Models
- **Related Terms**: slope, linear relationship, dependent variable

#### **Ionic Bond**
An attraction between an atom with a positive charge and an atom with a negative charge.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Chemical Bonds
- **Related Terms**: bond, covalent bond, ion

### K

#### **Kinase**
An enzyme that phosphorylates a protein.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: phosphorylation, phosphatase, enzyme, ATP

#### **Kinetic Energy**
Energy related to the motion of an atom or molecule.
- **Category**: Physics, Chemistry
- **Related Terms**: temperature, diffusion, thermal energy

### L

#### **Ligand**
A molecule that binds to a protein.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Molecular Interactions
- **Related Terms**: receptor, chemical affinity, chemical specificity

#### **Linear Relationship**
An association between the values of two variables that produces a straight line when graphed.
- **Category**: Statistics, Data Analysis
- **Related Terms**: slope, intercept, dependent variable, independent variable

### M

#### **Maximum**
The highest value in a set of numbers.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: minimum, mean, median

#### **Mean**
A parameter that defines the most likely value of a variable.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: median, standard deviation, frequency distribution

#### **Median**
The central value in an ordered set of numbers.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: mean, maximum, minimum

#### **Messenger RNA (mRNA)**
A molecule of RNA that contains the information needed to produce a peptide.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: RNA, transcription, translation, codon

#### **Minimum**
The lowest value in a set of numbers.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: maximum, mean, median

#### **Molecule**
A group of atoms held together by one or more covalent bonds.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Basic Concepts
- **Related Terms**: atom, covalent bond, compound

#### **Monomer**
A small molecule that bonds to similar molecules to form a polymer.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Biochemistry
- **Related Terms**: polymer, nucleotide, amino acid

#### **Mutation**
A substitution, insertion or deletion of nucleotides in a gene.
- **Category**: Genetics
- **Related Terms**: DNA, gene, evolutionary theory

### N

#### **Negative Feedback**
A regulatory process in which the product of one step in a sequence inhibits a previous step.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Homeostasis
- **Related Terms**: homeostasis, homeostatic system, regulation

#### **Net Diffusion**
A change in a concentration gradient caused by the movement of particles from a region of high concentration to a region of low concentration.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Transport
- **Related Terms**: diffusion, concentration gradient, Fick's equation

#### **Nucleic Acid**
A polymer of nucleotides.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: DNA, RNA, nucleotide, polymer

#### **Nucleoplasm**
The fluid inside the nucleus.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Cell Structure
- **Related Terms**: nucleus, cytoplasm, cell

#### **Nucleotide**
A molecule consisting of a phosphate, a carbohydrate, and a nitrogenous base.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: DNA, RNA, nucleic acid, monomer

#### **Nucleus**
A membrane-bound region of a cell that contains its DNA.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Cell Structure
- **Related Terms**: cell, nucleoplasm, DNA

### O

#### **Operon**
A cluster of genes controlled by a single promoter.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Regulation
- **Related Terms**: gene, promoter, transcription, regulatory element

#### **Osmolarity**
The total number of solutes per liter of solvent.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Solutions
- **Related Terms**: concentration, osmosis, solute, solvent

#### **Osmosis**
The net diffusion of water through a membrane.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Transport
- **Related Terms**: diffusion, water, membrane, osmolarity

### P

#### **Path Model**
A diagram of boxes and arrows depicting the causal relationships among variables in a system.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: direct effect, indirect effect, variable, system

#### **Peptide**
A polymer of two or more amino acids.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: protein, amino acid, peptide bond, polymer

#### **Peptide Bond**
A covalent bond between two amino acids.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: covalent bond, peptide, protein, amino acid

#### **Permeability**
A measure of how easily a solute passes through a membrane.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: cell membrane, diffusion, Fick's equation

#### **Phosphatase**
An enzyme that dephosphorylates a protein.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: dephosphorylation, kinase, enzyme, phosphorylation

#### **Phosphorylation**
A reaction that adds a phosphate group to a molecule.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: kinase, dephosphorylation, ATP, protein modification

#### **Polymer**
A large molecule composed of similar units bonded together.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Biochemistry
- **Related Terms**: monomer, protein, nucleic acid, carbohydrate

#### **Primary Active Transport**
Using energy in the form of ATP to move a solute from a region of low concentration to a region of high concentration.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: active transport, ATP, concentration gradient, pump

#### **Promoter**
A region of DNA where RNA polymerase can bind and initiate transcription.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Expression
- **Related Terms**: transcription, RNA polymerase, gene, operon

#### **Protein**
A large molecule consisting of one or more chains of repeated elements called amino acids.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: peptide, amino acid, polymer, enzyme

### R

#### **Reasoning**
The process of logically relating evidence to a claim.
- **Category**: Scientific Method
- **Related Terms**: evidence, claim, scientific argument

#### **Receptor**
A protein that binds specific signaling molecules and changes conformation to initiate a response.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: ligand, signal transduction, cellular response

#### **Regulatory Element**
A region of DNA where a protein can bind and affect the probability of transcription.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Regulation
- **Related Terms**: activator, repressor, transcription factor, promoter

#### **Relationship**
An association between the values of two variables, such that the expected value of one variable depends on the observed value of the other variable.
- **Category**: Statistics, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: dependent variable, independent variable, linear relationship

#### **Relative Frequency**
The proportion or percentage of times that a value within a range was observed.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: frequency, frequency distribution, probability

#### **Repressor**
A transcription factor that binds to a regulatory element and prevents transcription.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Regulation
- **Related Terms**: activator, transcription factor, regulatory element

#### **Residual**
The quantity remaining after some value has been subtracted.
- **Category**: Statistics, Data Analysis
- **Related Terms**: residual sum of squares, linear model, error

#### **Residual Sum of Squares**
A measure of the deviations between observed values of a dependent variable and the expected values determined by a linear model.
- **Category**: Statistics, Data Analysis
- **Related Terms**: residual, sum of squares, linear model

#### **Ribonucleic Acid (RNA)**
A nucleic acid in which each nucleotide has ribose as its carbohydrate.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: DNA, nucleic acid, mRNA, tRNA, rRNA

#### **Ribosome**
A complex of RNA and proteins that translates a molecule of mRNA into a polymer of amino acids.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: translation, mRNA, tRNA, protein synthesis

#### **RNA Polymerase**
An enzyme that unwinds DNA and produces a molecule of RNA with a complementary sequence of nucleotides.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: transcription, DNA, RNA, promoter

### S

#### **Scientific Argument**
A chain of reasoning that connects evidence to a claim.
- **Category**: Scientific Method
- **Related Terms**: claim, evidence, reasoning

#### **Secondary Active Transport**
Using energy in the form of an electrochemical gradient to move a solute from a region of low concentration to a region of high concentration.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: active transport, electrochemical gradient, symporter, antiporter

#### **Secondary Messenger**
A molecule in the cell activated when a signaling molecule binds to a receptor in the membrane.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: signal transduction, receptor, signaling pathway

#### **Sensor**
A component that detects or measures a physical property.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Homeostasis
- **Related Terms**: homeostatic system, integrator, effector

#### **Set Point**
The target value of a regulated variable.
- **Category**: Systems Biology, Homeostasis
- **Related Terms**: homeostasis, negative feedback, regulation

#### **Signal Transduction**
A process in which a receptor transmits a signal from a signaling molecule to other molecules inside a cell.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: receptor, signaling pathway, cellular response

#### **Signaling Pathway**
A set of chemical reactions through which molecules in a cell coordinate a response to a signaling molecule.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: signal transduction, cellular response, amplification

#### **Slope**
A parameter that defines how a change in the value of the independent variable affects the expected value of the dependent variable.
- **Category**: Statistics, Linear Models
- **Related Terms**: intercept, linear relationship, regression

#### **Solute**
An ion or molecule dissolved in a solvent.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Solutions
- **Related Terms**: solvent, solution, concentration, osmolarity

#### **Solvent**
A liquid that dissolves ions or molecules to create a solution.
- **Category**: Chemistry, Solutions
- **Related Terms**: solute, solution, water

#### **Standard Deviation**
A parameter that represents the uncertainty in the value of a variable.
- **Category**: Statistics
- **Related Terms**: mean, variance, frequency distribution

#### **Substrate**
A molecule that participates in a chemical reaction involving an enzyme.
- **Category**: Biochemistry, Enzymes
- **Related Terms**: enzyme, active site, product, catalyst

#### **Sum of Squares**
A measure of the deviations between observed values of a variable and the mean.
- **Category**: Statistics, Data Analysis
- **Related Terms**: variance, standard deviation, residual sum of squares

#### **Symporter**
A carrier protein that binds two or more types of solutes and changes conformation to move them through the membrane in the same direction.
- **Category**: Cell Biology, Membrane Transport
- **Related Terms**: antiporter, carrier protein, secondary active transport

#### **System**
A set of components that interact as a network. The structure of a system determines its function.
- **Category**: Systems Biology
- **Related Terms**: homeostatic system, path model, network

### T

#### **Temperature**
A measure of the mean kinetic energy of a system.
- **Category**: Physics, Chemistry
- **Related Terms**: kinetic energy, diffusion, heat

#### **Termination**
A process that deactivates the molecules in a signaling pathway.
- **Category**: Cell Signaling
- **Related Terms**: signaling pathway, signal transduction, regulation

#### **Transcription**
A process in which an enzyme uses DNA to produce a molecule of RNA with a complementary sequence of nucleotides.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Expression
- **Related Terms**: RNA polymerase, DNA, mRNA, gene expression

#### **Transcription Factor**
A protein that affects the probability that RNA polymerase will transcribe a gene.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Regulation
- **Related Terms**: activator, repressor, regulatory element, promoter

#### **Transfer RNA (tRNA)**
A molecule of RNA that transfers an amino acid to a ribosome.
- **Category**: Genetics, Molecular Biology
- **Related Terms**: translation, ribosome, amino acid, codon

#### **Translation**
A process in which a cell uses mRNA to produce a polymer of amino acids.
- **Category**: Genetics, Gene Expression
- **Related Terms**: ribosome, mRNA, tRNA, protein synthesis

### V

A characteristic or quantity that changes according to the situation.
- **Category**: Statistics, Research Methods
- **Related Terms**: dependent variable, independent variable, relationship

#### **Variance**
A measure of how spread out values are from their mean; changes according to the situation.
