## Course Overview

### Course Description
**BIO 181: General Biology I** is a comprehensive introduction to biological principles at the molecular and cellular levels. This integrated lecture and laboratory course covers biological macromolecules, cells, membranes, metabolism, energy production, molecular biology, genetics, and recombinant DNA technology. The course is designed for life sciences majors and includes hands-on laboratory experiences using state-of-the-art Dreamscape Learn Virtual Reality technology.

### Course Components
- **Lecture:** 3 credit hours
- **Laboratory:** 1 credit hour (linked to lecture)
- **Total Credits:** 4
- **Prerequisites:** C or better in BIO 181, BIO 281, or BIO 282, OR Visiting University Student status
- **Note:** Credit allowed for only BIO 181 or BIO 281 or BIO 282

### Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Scientific Reasoning and Process**
   - Apply scientific methodologies to novel problems
   - Design experiments with proper controls
   - Analyze and interpret experimental data
   - Distinguish between observations, inferences, and assumptions

2. **Biological Molecules and Chemistry**
   - Describe structures and functions of biological macromolecules
   - Explain how molecular structure determines function
   - Predict effects of environmental changes on molecular structures

3. **Cell Biology**
   - Compare and contrast prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
   - Describe functions of cellular organelles and structures
   - Explain membrane structure and transport mechanisms
   - Analyze cell communication and signaling pathways

4. **Energy and Metabolism**
   - Explain energy transformations in biological systems
   - Describe cellular respiration and photosynthesis pathways
   - Apply thermodynamics principles to biological processes

5. **Genetics and Molecular Biology**
   - Describe DNA replication, transcription, and translation
   - Apply Mendelian genetics principles
   - Analyze gene expression and regulation mechanisms
   - Understand recombinant DNA technology applications

6. **Laboratory Skills**
   - Use proper laboratory techniques and safety protocols
   - Operate scientific equipment including microscopes
   - Collect, analyze, and present scientific data
   - Work effectively in collaborative research teams

## Laboratory Information

### Dreamscape Learn Virtual Reality

The laboratory component utilizes state-of-the-art Dreamscape Learn (DSL) Virtual Reality technology, providing immersive, story-driven experiences that allow students to solve real-world biological problems firsthand.

#### DSL Modules

1. **Scientific Reasoning Module**
   - Act I: Late Arrival (Introduction to experimental design)
   - Act II: A Big Decision (Hypothesis testing and controls)
   - Act III: The Hatching (Data analysis and conclusions)

2. **Cell Biology Module**
   - Act I: A Mysterious Disease (Cell structure and function)
   - Act II: An Unusual Diagnosis (Cellular processes and metabolism)
   - Act III: The Cure (Applications of cell biology)

3. **Physiology Module**
   - Act I: Off Course (Organismal physiology basics)
   - Act II: A Weighty Problem (Homeostasis and regulation)
   - Act III: The Journey (Integration of physiological systems)
