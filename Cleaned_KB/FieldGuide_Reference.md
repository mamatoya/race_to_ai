## ALLURIA

### Shimmerfish
**Scientific Name:** *Piscadae lucida*  
**Avg. Size:** 15-25 cm in length  
**Avg. Mass:** 200-400 g  
**Lifespan:** 3-5 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Aquatic

**Description:** The shimmerfish is a small, iridescent fish found in the crystal-clear lakes of Alluria. Its scales reflect light in a dazzling array of colors, creating a mesmerizing display as schools swim together. The shimmerfish has large, sensitive eyes adapted for detecting subtle changes in light patterns, which helps it communicate with others of its species and avoid predators.

**Habitat:** Shimmerfish inhabit the shallow, sunlit waters of Alluria's many lakes. They prefer areas with abundant aquatic vegetation where they can hide from predators and find food. These fish are most active during the day when sunlight penetrates the water, enhancing their shimmering appearance.

**Diet:** Shimmerfish are omnivores, feeding on small aquatic insects, algae, and plant matter. They use their small, pointed mouths to pick food from surfaces and capture tiny prey items in the water column.

**Reproduction:** Shimmerfish spawn during Alluria's warm season. Females lay adhesive eggs on aquatic plants, which males then fertilize. The eggs hatch within 7-10 days, and the young fish form schools for protection as they grow.

**Interesting Fact:** Shimmerfish can produce light through bioluminescence when stressed or during mating displays, adding an extra layer of visual communication to their already dazzling appearance.

### Moorhen
**Scientific Name:** *Gallinula nebulae*  
**Avg. Size:** 35-40 cm in length  
**Avg. Mass:** 300-450 g  
**Lifespan:** 8-10 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Semi-aquatic

**Description:** The moorhen is a medium-sized bird with dark plumage that has an iridescent quality. Its most distinctive feature is its bright red bill and frontal shield. Moorhens have long, green legs with large feet that help them walk on floating vegetation.

**Habitat:** Moorhens are found in marshy areas and along the edges of lakes and slow-moving streams on Alluria. They build their nests in dense vegetation near water and are territorial during breeding season.

**Diet:** These birds are omnivores, eating aquatic plants, seeds, small fish, insects, and crustaceans. They often feed while swimming or walking along the water's edge.

**Reproduction:** Moorhens form monogamous pairs during breeding season. They build floating nests anchored to vegetation and can have multiple broods per year. Both parents care for the precocial chicks.

**Interesting Fact:** Moorhens have a unique "flick" display where they rapidly flash the white undertail coverts as a warning signal to others.

### Crystal Crab
**Scientific Name:** *Crystallomorpha transparens*  
**Avg. Size:** 10-15 cm across carapace  
**Avg. Mass:** 150-300 g  
**Lifespan:** 12-15 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Amphibious

**Description:** The crystal crab has a translucent exoskeleton that refracts light, creating rainbow patterns across its shell. Its body structure allows observers to see internal organs, giving it an ethereal appearance. The crab has specialized light-sensing organs along its legs.

**Habitat:** Crystal crabs inhabit the tidal zones of Alluria's crystalline beaches, where quartz-like sand meets the water. They create burrows in the sand that fill with water during high tide.

**Diet:** These crabs are scavengers and filter feeders, consuming detritus, small organisms, and organic matter filtered from the sand and water.

**Reproduction:** Crystal crabs perform elaborate mating dances during full moons, using their translucent bodies to create light shows. Females carry eggs under their abdomen until they hatch into planktonic larvae.

**Interesting Fact:** Crystal crabs can temporarily change the refractive index of their shells to become nearly invisible when threatened.

### Wind Dancer
**Scientific Name:** *Aerobates gracilis*  
**Avg. Size:** 2 m wingspan  
**Avg. Mass:** 3-4 kg  
**Lifespan:** 20-25 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Aerial

**Description:** Wind Dancers are elegant flying creatures with elongated, ribbon-like wings that undulate as they fly. Their bodies are slender and streamlined, covered in fine, silk-like feathers that shimmer in the sunlight. They have long, trailing tail feathers that help them perform intricate aerial maneuvers.

**Habitat:** These creatures spend most of their lives airborne, riding the thermal currents above Alluria's mountains and valleys. They only land to nest on high cliff faces and mountain peaks.

**Diet:** Wind Dancers feed on aerial plankton, small flying insects, and pollen carried on wind currents. They filter feed while flying with their specialized beak structure.

**Reproduction:** Wind Dancers perform elaborate courtship flights that can last for hours. They build minimal nests on cliff ledges and raise one chick at a time, teaching it complex flying techniques.

**Interesting Fact:** Wind Dancers can sleep while flying, shutting down half their brain at a time while maintaining flight patterns.

### Glowmoss
**Scientific Name:** *Bryophyta luminosa*  
**Avg. Size:** Forms patches 1-3 m in diameter  
**Avg. Mass:** Variable  
**Lifespan:** Colony can live indefinitely  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** Glowmoss is a bioluminescent moss that covers rocks and tree trunks in Alluria's forests. During the day, it appears as ordinary green moss, but at night it emits a soft, blue-green glow. The intensity of the glow varies with humidity and temperature.

**Habitat:** Glowmoss thrives in the humid understory of Alluria's temperate forests, particularly in areas with consistent moisture and shade.

**Diet:** As a photosynthetic organism, glowmoss produces its own food from sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. It also absorbs nutrients from the surfaces it grows on.

**Reproduction:** Glowmoss reproduces through spores that are released during rainfall. The bioluminescence intensifies during spore release, creating spectacular light displays in the forest.

**Interesting Fact:** The bioluminescence of glowmoss follows a circadian rhythm and can synchronize across entire forest patches, creating waves of light.

**Avg. Mass:** 2-3 g  
**Lifespan:** 30-40 days as adult  
**Lifestyle:** Aerial/Terrestrial

**Habitat:** Prism butterflies are found in Alluria's meadows and gardens, particularly areas with abundant flowering plants.

**Diet:** Adults feed on nectar from flowers, while caterpillars eat specific host plants found in Allurian meadows.

**Reproduction:** Females lay eggs on host plants. The caterpillars pupate in crystalline cocoons that reflect light, providing camouflage.

**Interesting Fact:** Prism butterflies navigate using polarized light patterns invisible to most other creatures.

### Singing Reed
**Scientific Name:** *Melodicus ventus*  
**Avg. Size:** 2-4 m tall  
**Avg. Mass:** 5-10 kg per cluster  
**Lifespan:** 5-8 Earth years per stem  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** Singing reeds are tall, hollow plants that produce musical tones when wind passes through them. Each reed has a unique pitch based on its length and diameter. Clusters of reeds create harmonious chords that change with wind patterns.

**Habitat:** These plants grow in wetlands and along riverbanks on Alluria, forming dense stands that can cover several hectares.

**Diet:** Singing reeds are photosynthetic plants that also absorb nutrients through their extensive root systems in wetland soils.

**Reproduction:** They reproduce both through seeds dispersed by wind and through underground rhizomes that produce new shoots.

**Interesting Fact:** Local wildlife has learned to use the different tones produced by singing reeds as navigation aids and territorial markers.

## CADMUS

### Griff
**Scientific Name:** *Dalidali*  
**Avg. Size:** up to 178 cm in height for females, 186 cm for males (to the top of the head), antlers can grow up to 41 cm in length  
**Avg. Mass:** 900 kg  
**Lifespan:** 18 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** Male griffs have large colorful sacs and fans that frill out from the neck. They can manipulate these growths and use them to create dazzling mating displays for the females. Females are smaller in build and drabber in color. They do exhibit unique striations along their backsides. Every two years a new stripe appears which allows for easy determination of their age.

**Habitat:** The Griff is a large herbivorous creature that inhabits the steppes of Cadmus. The steppes are largely arid with dispersed lakes that provide the water source for the griffs. They range through areas that are sparsely populated with vegetation and remain largely dispersed as a population.

**Diet:** Griffs consume vegetation that grows sparsely among the steppes of Cadmus. They use their highly flexible mouths to manipulate vegetation. Flattened tooth-like structures enable the griff to chew down the toughest of vegetations. Because the griff's diet is limited in a relatively arid region, they supplement their diet by using their mouth plates to scrape salt and mineral formations at the edges of lakes.

**Reproduction:** During the mating season, males and females congregate in an elaborate mating ritual called a lek. The males put on elaborate shows of agility and color in an attempt to attract a female. However, females are hard to impress and the males compete with each other to do so. If a male is successful, the female will become receptive to mating. After the mating event, the males and females disperse again. Females typically give birth to one offspring. Baby griffs are born walking and will follow their mothers around until 5 months of age at which time they are ready to breed.

**Interesting Fact:** Both the males and females are able to accurately navigate to their original breeding grounds for the annual mating ritual based solely on environmental cues.

### Kolvock
**Scientific Name:** *Sasquali*  
**Avg. Size:** 480 cm tall  
**Avg. Mass:** 5800 kg  
**Lifespan:** 180 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** The kolvock is a large organism characterized by their tough rocky hides and odd two-legged body plan. They blend into the rocky areas of Cadmus and can be indistinguishable from their surrounding environment. The long snout of the kolvock houses a formidable mouth that can intake large amounts of organic matter. Their tongue can extend well outside the mouth to acquire water from almost any source.

**Habitat:** Kolvocks roams about the steppes of Cadmus and can survive in very arid environments with minimal access to water.

**Diet:** Kolvocks are omnivores, eating a wide range of organic matter. They roam the steppe looking for carcasses, plant matter and anything they can eat. The large mouth of the kolvock is used for rummaging through dirt and digging up roots, swallowing bones and snapping up smaller organisms. These creatures have been nicknamed "garbage disposals."

**Reproduction:** Little is known about the mating habits of the kolvock. It's been observed that females will dig a nest in which they lay about 30 eggs.

**Interesting Fact:** Kolvocks are one of the oldest and longest-living creatures on Cadmus with a genetic line that spans tens of millions of years. They are virtually unchanged from a morphological standpoint and therefore adaptable to rapid environmental shifts.

### Skyray
**Scientific Name:** *Mixitali*  
**Avg. Size:** 90 cm wingspan  
**Avg. Mass:** 4 kg  
**Lifespan:** 9 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Aerial

**Description:** Skyrays are characterized by their large wingspan and whip-like tails. The skyrays are very graceful fliers appearing to almost swim through the air as they fly. The sky ray possesses three small claws along the underside of their body with which they grip to cliff sides. They travel in pods of 6-15 individuals and fly in very intricate patterns within the group.

**Habitat:** Sky Rays are large airborne creatures that glide on air currents high above the Cadmian steppes.

**Diet:** Skyrays filter feed on vegetation, pollen, and seeds that float in the sky. The large prongs along the sides of the head help guide food into their mouth. They can be found floating on wind currents as air is filtered through their mouths.

**Reproduction:** Skyrays use a communal breeding technique in which females will lay clusters of eggs in groups along cliff sides. The eggs are extremely sticky and will remain anchored for 3 weeks. In that time, visiting males will fertilize the eggs. Once eggs are fertilized, they take 2 weeks to gestate. Newborn sky rays are only about 2 inches big and are essentially taken up into the lower atmosphere by strong summer winds. There, they will grow into adults and eventually descend into the lower skies.

**Interesting Fact:** A large gas sac on their head that can be filled or emptied with low density gas that helps them maintain their position in different layers of the atmosphere along with their broad wings.

### Nexus Grass
**Scientific Name:** *[Data not available]*  
**Avg. Size:** [Data not available]  
**Avg. Mass:** [Data not available]  
**Lifespan:** [Data not available]  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** Nexus Grass is a remarkable species of vegetation that forms vast meadows. At first glance, it appears like typical grass with slender emerald-green blades that sway gracefully in the wind. However, upon closer inspection, faint bioluminescent veins can be observed running through each blade. These veins pulse with a soft, rhythmic glow, particularly visible during twilight hours. The root system of Nexus Grass is equally extraordinary, forming an intricate underground network through specialized fungal partnerships (mycorrhizae) that connect individual plants across great distances.

**Habitat:** Nexus Grass thrives in the temperate meadows and prairies, where it forms expansive, interconnected fields. It prefers well-drained soils and areas with moderate rainfall. The grass can adapt to various elevations, from lowland plains to mountain meadows, as long as the climate remains relatively stable.

**Diet:** Like most plants, Nexus Grass primarily derives energy through photosynthesis. However, its extensive mycorrhizal network allows it to share nutrients with other plants in the meadow. This creates a cooperative ecosystem where nutrients are distributed based on need.

**Reproduction:** Nexus Grass reproduction is an intricate and collaborative process within the collective intelligence network. During the planet's cyclic seasons, the grass experiences a phase of accelerated growth and vitality. In a rare reproductive strategy, certain specialized grass blades take on the role of "seed-bearers." These blades produce small, translucent seed pods that contain genetic information encoded within them. When conditions are optimal, the seed bearers retreat into the soil. The mycelium network then plays a crucial role in distributing these seeds to various parts of the meadow, ensuring genetic diversity throughout the grassland. New grass blades sprout from these seeds, and the cycle continues, strengthening the collective intelligence with each new generation.

**Interesting Fact:** Nexus Grass can communicate through subtle vibrations. The grass blades can be seen gently swaying in rhythm, creating mesmerizing patterns across the meadow. These synchronized movements and vibrations are not just aesthetic; they serve as a form of non-verbal communication to other distant collective intelligence networks.

## PHYGARIS

### Seezo
**Scientific Name:** *Multiceii*  
**Avg. Size:** up to 60 cm in height, tail extends to 170 cm  
**Avg. Mass:** 86 kg  
**Lifespan:** 15-18 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** The Seezo is a land-based arthropod found in the hilly grasslands of Phygaris. The Seezo's most distinctive trait are its four eyes located on stalks. These eyes help the Seezo spot predators and prey from great distances. Additionally, Seezo's are very fast runners.

**Habitat:** The Seezo lives in the hilly grasslands of Phygaris. They tunnel down to create elaborate underground nests that can extend for meters and include multiple chambers.

**Diet:** The Seezo uses its long snout to consume vegetation and small organisms. The Seezo uses its whiskers to feel around in underground caverns that it cannot fit its entire body through.

**Reproduction:** Seezo reproduce sexually once a year. Females lay sacks of hundreds of eggs which the male fertilizes. Once fertilized they bury them underground. When ready, the eggs hatch and the Seezo adolescents tunnel their way to the surface. Only about 5% of all eggs survive to adulthood.

**Interesting Fact:** The Seezo communicates through the movement of their four eyestalks. They have a complex signaling behavior through movements that researchers have been trying to understand for decades.

### Belldrop
**Scientific Name:** *Melodicii*  
**Avg. Size:** 80 cm tall  
**Avg. Mass:** 5 kg  
**Lifespan:** 5 Earth years  
**Lifestyle:** Terrestrial

**Description:** The Belldrop is a bell-shaped flower that has one single petal. The petal varies in color with hues of purple and blue. The petal is attached to its cylindrical green stem by a hinge at the base of the flower.

**Habitat:** The Belldrop grows in the forest floors of Phygaris. It grows in large clusters in areas of little sunlight.

**Diet:** The Belldrop performs photosynthesis. Its large, efficient petal can capture any amount of light, even in the darkest areas of the forest floor.

**Reproduction:** When the Belldrop is ready to reproduce it produces pollen on the inside of its bell-shaped petal. The Belldrop can close its petal. When the wind blows with enough strength, the petal flips back and forth making a deep bellowing sound. The pollen is shaken off the petal and blown away in the wind. If any of the pollen lands on another belldrop, the belldrops can sexually reproduce.

**Interesting Fact:** The beautiful sounds of the Belldrops can be heard seasonally when the winds blow. The pitch of the tone made varies depending on the shape and thickness of the petal. Older flowers make a deeper sound than the younger, lighter ones. The forests during these times are loud but melodic. Many creatures find the sounds soothing.

**Diet:** Elans are herbivores that browse on leaves, shoots, and grasses. They can go without water for extended periods, getting most of their moisture from the vegetation they eat.

**Reproduction:** Elans form small herds with a dominant male. Mating occurs during the wet season. After a gestation of 8 months, females give birth to a single calf that can walk within an hour of birth.

**Interesting Fact:** Elans can leap up to 3 meters high and 10 meters horizontally from a standing position, an ability that helps them escape predators.
